William B. Stout

1935 SAE President

William B. Stout was an engineer, inventor, speaker, and designer with experience in both the automotive and aviation fields. Stout worked for the Packard Motor Company in 1916 before he started the Stout Engineering Company, known for building prototypes like the Stout Scarab car in 1932. In 1934, he founded the Stout Motor Car Company.
